Who We Are

In our experience it can be difficult to find useful resources for chemistry and computer science. It may take hours for us to find a site that further explores our areas of expertise with reliable and credible information. Google is an incredible tool that will greatly expedite the search for information, but it is only useful if you know what you are searching for. Our website is designed so that a variety of useful resources, some you may not have even known you needed, can all be found in one spot. This will allow others to avoid the problems that we encountered. Every resource found on our website is one that we have used in the past and would recommend to anyone else in our field.

Chemistry

I chose each of these links based on how useful they are. Some of them I have used extensively and others I have heard good reviews from my teachers. My sources include chemical databases, chemical calculators, educational platforms, reaction tables, unit converters, scientific articles, forums, and several periodic tables to choose from. As I discover more useful websites I will add them to my sources.

Computer Science

I personally had a fun time going through each source I saved and determining if they should be a part of this list. My criteria for wading through the good sources vs the bad was: the amount of times I used them, how many people use them, and how easy it is for someone to find valuable information off them. Hence why this table include Java and C++ documentation, but not ACL2. I also included sites that are commonly used by web developers in order to make applications or websites as most people, like me, want to code something functional.
